Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 8, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Signyx ("we," "our," or "us") collects, uses, and handles information when you use the Signyx mobile application (the "App").

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information Collected Automatically

Network Usage Data

The App reads network traffic statistics from your device's operating system (via Android's TrafficStats API) to display per-app bandwidth usage. This data is processed locally on your device and is never transmitted to our servers.

Location Information

On Android 8.1 and later, displaying your Wi-Fi network name (SSID) requires the ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION permission. We use this permission solely to retrieve your current Wi-Fi network name. We do not track your physical location, store GPS coordinates, or transmit location data off your device.

Network Scan Data

When you use the Network Scanner feature, the App scans your local Wi-Fi network to discover connected devices. This produces a list of IP addresses, MAC addresses, hostnames, and response times visible on your network. This data is stored locally on your device only and is never uploaded to external servers. Premium users who enable Unknown Device Alerts will have scans run periodically in the background; results remain local.

Speed Test Data

When you run a speed test, the App connects to Cloudflare's speed test infrastructure (speed.cloudflare.com) to measure your download speed, upload speed, and latency. Speed test results are stored locally on your device. Cloudflare may collect connection metadata as part of this request; please refer to Cloudflare's privacy policy for details.

Crash and Diagnostic Data

If the App crashes, Firebase Crashlytics automatically collects an anonymised crash report. This report may include your device model, Android OS version, App version, and a stack trace describing the crash location. It does not include your name, email address, location, or any data displayed within the App (speeds, scan results, usage stats). Crash data is used solely to identify and fix bugs.

Device Identifiers

The App does not directly collect your device's IMEI or phone number. Advertising identifiers may be accessed by our advertising partner (see Section 3) for free-tier users who have provided consent.

1.2 Information You Provide

The App does not require you to create an account. You may optionally set a daily data usage limit and notification preferences within the App. These preferences are stored locally on your device using Android's DataStore.

2. How We Use Information

All network usage data, location information, scan results, and speed test history are processed and stored exclusively on your device. We use this information solely to:

  • Display real-time and historical network speed and usage statistics
  • Show per-app bandwidth consumption
  • Run and store speed test results
  • Present network scan results and unknown device alerts (Premium)
  • Generate weekly PDF reports of your speed test history (Premium)
  • Deliver notifications about your network status
  • Identify and fix crashes via anonymised crash reports (Crashlytics)

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for marketing purposes.

3. Third-Party Services

3.1 Google AdMob (Free tier only)

The free version of the App displays advertisements served by Google AdMob. AdMob may collect device identifiers and usage data to serve personalised or non-personalised ads, subject to your consent choices presented within the App.

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, and Switzerland, we collect your consent via Google's User Messaging Platform before initialising AdMob. You may change your ad preferences at any time in the App's Settings.

Google's privacy policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

3.2 Google Play Billing

Premium subscriptions are processed through Google Play Billing. Payment information is handled entirely by Google; we never receive or store your payment card details. Purchase records (subscription status) are provided to us by Google Play to verify your premium entitlement.

Google Play's terms: https://play.google.com/about/play-terms/

3.3 Firebase Crashlytics (Google)

We use Firebase Crashlytics to automatically collect anonymised crash reports when the App encounters an unexpected error. Reports may include device model, OS version, App version, and a stack trace. No personally identifiable information is included. Crashlytics data is governed by Google's Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

3.4 Cloudflare

Speed tests route a data transfer through Cloudflare's servers. Cloudflare may log connection metadata (IP address, timestamp, bytes transferred). We do not receive this log data.

Cloudflare's privacy policy: https://www.cloudflare.com/privacypolicy/

4. Data Storage and Security

All data collected by the App (usage history, scan results, speed test history, preferences) is stored locally in an encrypted database on your device using Room (SQLite) and Android DataStore. We do not operate servers that store your personal data. Uninstalling the App permanently removes all locally stored data.

5. Data Retention

Locally stored data is retained until you uninstall the App or manually clear the App's data in your device settings. Free users' speed test history is automatically limited to the 10 most recent results. Premium users retain unlimited history locally. Crash reports are retained by Firebase Crashlytics for 90 days per Google's standard policy.

7. Children's Privacy

The App is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided personal information through the App, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

8. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Request correction or deletion of your data
  • Withdraw consent for data processing
  • Lodge a complaint with a data protection authority

Because all your data is stored locally on your device, you can exercise most of these rights directly by clearing the App's data or uninstalling it. For crash report data held by Firebase, you may contact us to request deletion. For all other requests, contact us at signyx.support@gmail.com.
